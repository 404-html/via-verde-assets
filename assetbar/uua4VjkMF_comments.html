bjorntd » neu 3 years ago
Dropped gerunds are typical in Ebonics, which Teodor is mocking here. A dropped gerund is a gerund (words like running jumping etc) minus the "ing" suffix, so, for example, "I've got to tip the Panamanian boy who is vacuuming the gerunds from the Escalade's carpet" becomes "I got to tip the little Panama boy what vacuums my dropped gerunds out of the bottom of my Escalade."
